Me and the Bicycle

I still remember the first time I saw it. It was old, a little rusty, and the paint was chipped in places—but to me, it was beautiful. A bicycle that felt like it had a story of its own.

I didn't have much. But every time I looked at that bike, I felt a dream growing quietly in my chest. I imagined riding down the hill near my home, the wind brushing against my face, the world flying by as if nothing could touch me. I wanted that more than anything.

It took weeks of saving, skipping snacks, and doing small chores for neighbors. Finally, the day came. I handed over the coins, one by one, to the man who had kept it for me. He smiled, maybe because he knew what it meant.

The first ride was wobbly. I fell more than once. My hands were scratched, my knees bruised—but my heart? It was full. That bike became more than just a way to get around. It became my escape, my freedom, my little piece of happiness in a noisy world.

Even now, when I pass by the place where I used to ride, I remember that feeling. The simple joy of holding onto something I worked for. The quiet lesson that sometimes, the smallest things—like an old bicycle—can carry our biggest dreams.

It wasn’t just a bike. It was a memory, a friend, and a symbol of who I was becoming.
